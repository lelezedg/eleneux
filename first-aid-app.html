Case Study 

First Aid APP

Check the Prototype

Overview

The Product

The app is designed for people who are interested in first-aid. The First Aid app will teach you how to handle yourself and injured people and how to behave during floods, earthquakes, and other natural disasters. The aims to provide useful information and make the learning process easier and pleasurable.

The Problem

Lots of people still don't know how to behave during emergencies. Most of them have never taught first aid and are unable to handle those situations.

My Role

UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery.

Project Duration

July 2021 to September 2021

The Goal

Our goal is to educate people on how to behave during a natural disaster or how to handle injuries.

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews,
paper and digital wireframing,
low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility,
iterating on designs,
Information architecture.

Understanding 

The User

I've conducted user interviews and got lots of insights. Most interview participants reported that they don't have enough information about first aid, and never had the opportunity to learn.

The feedback received through research made it clear, that users are willing to get basic information on how to behave during an emergency if they had an easy-to-use tool to help them.

Persona & Problem Statement

Stella 

is a part time graphic designer and Mom, who needs to find a platform that she will use in teaching her kid first aid.

Jennifer 

is a full-time student who needs an app that teaches her how to handle emergencies to get used to living alone.

Starting 

The Design

After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the First aid app. These designs focus on delivering personalized guidance to users to help them learn to handle emergencies.

Usability Study Findings

To prepare for testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype and used this prototype to conduct an unmoderated usability study with 7 participants. Here are the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

People would like to read more illustrated articles

Just reading the topics won't be interesting for the users

Users need the motivation to use this application

Refining

The Design

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied some changes, I’ve added tests section because of the users’ needs

Provided illustrations make the article interesting and visually appealing. Texts are simplified and easy to read even for the children

Accessibility Consideration

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers

The colors are specially selected and the contrast is checked

I've used headings with different sized text for clear visual hierarchy

Responsive 

Design

With the app designs completed, I started work on designing the responsive website. I used the First aid app’s sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen’s design to ensure a cohesive and consistent experience across devices.

Going Forward 

Takeaways

Impact

Users shared that this First aid app really gives some important information, simplifies the studying process using the tests, and they will definitely use it.

What I learned

I learned that even though the problem I was trying to solve was a big one, diligently going through each step of the design process and aligning with specific user needs helped me come up with solutions that were both feasible and useful.

Next Steps

Conduct another round of usability studies on how successful it is

Add more educational resources for users to learn about first aid

Conduct more research to integrate new useful features
