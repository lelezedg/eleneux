Case Study 

First Aid

App & Responsive Website

Overview

The Product

The application is designed for people who are interested in first-aid practices. The First Aid app will teach you how to handle yourself and injured people during floods, earthquakes, and other natural disasters. Also, it has the most popular myths about first aid.

The Problem

Lots of people still don't know how to behave during emergency situations or natural disasters. Most of them have never taught first aid and are unable to handle the situations.

My Role

UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery

Project Duration

July 2021 to September 2021

The Goal

Our goal is to educate people on how to behave during a natural disaster or how to handle physical injuries. We aim to reduce first aid myths.

Responsibilities

Conducting interviews,
paper and digital wireframing,
low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility,
iterating on designs,
Information architecture,
responsive design.

Understanding 

The User

I've conducted user interviews and got lots of insights. Most interview participants reported that they don't have enough information about first aid, and never had the opportunity to learn.

The feedback received through research made it very clear that users would be open and willing to get basic information on how to behave during emergency situations if they had an easy-to-use tool to help guide them.

Persona & Problem Statement

Stella 

is a part-time graphic designer and mom, who needs to find a platform that she will use in teaching her kid first aid.

Jennifer 

is a full-time student who needs an app that teaches her how to handle emergencies to get used to living alone.

Starting 

The Design

After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the First aid app. These designs focused on delivering personalized guidance to users to help learn handling emergencies.

Usability Study Findings

To prepare for testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype which you can view here. I used this prototype to conduct an unmoderated usability study with 7 participants. Here are the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

People would like to read more illustrated articles

Users would like to have access to News in the app

Users need search button to navigate easily

Refining

The Design

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes, I’ve added search button because of the users’ needs

I've decided to change the orientation of the categories to make sure that users can navigate easily using different devices.

Responsive 

Design

With the app designs completed, I started work on designing the responsive website. I used the First aid app’s sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen’s design to ensure a cohesive and consistent experience across devices.

Sitemap

Responsive Designs

The designs for screen size variation included mobile, tablet, and desktop. I optimized the designs to fit specific user needs of each device and screen size.

Going Forward 

Takeaways

Impact

Users shared that this First aid app really gives some important information and they will definitely use it.

What I learned

I learned that even though the problem I was trying to solve was a big one, diligently going through each step of the design process and aligning with specific user needs helped me come up with solutions that were both feasible and useful.

Next Steps

Conduct another round of usability studies on how successful the app is

Add more educational resources for users to learn about first aid

Conduct more research to integrate new useful features into the application
